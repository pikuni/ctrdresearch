 A PANORAMIC VIEW IN ORGANISING THE SEMINAR :

The liberalization of marketing process and globalization and co modification of goods and services has not only changed the market scenario in rural areas but also rural consumerism.  The problems relating to rights of rural consumer are numerous and complex.  During last couple of years, the empowerment spree of rural India is on its high due to which the economy pertaining to this section is showing impressive growth. The propellers for this progress include government initiatives and schemes, infrastructure development, industry projects across the country and the emphasis on local-employability.  The rural consumers now-a-days  are developing desire for packaged foods, personal care products, consumer durables and IT products, two- and four-wheelers, and fashion accessories.

The following factors have radically changed the behavior and attitude of rural consumers for last couple of years. 

  • Rural purchasing power has grown faster than urban
  • Faster growth in rural is not limited to penetration; today the rural consumer’s frequency of consumption is growing faster as well, demonstrating their entrenchment in these categories
  • The rural consumers prefer to purchase deodorant and fabric softener much higher rate in rural India than urban

There is an urgent need to critically examine different issues and challenges encountered by Rural Consumers and initiate appropriate measures for protecting the rights of the consumers.  The Consumer Organisations and Consumer Protection in rural area is a major thrust area for developing appropriate redressal measure at the back drop of Consumer Protection Act, 1986-Ligislative and Judicial Dimensions.  Consumer Behaviour, Communication and Rural Market play an important role in Rural Consumerism.  Buying Behaviour and Grievance Redressal in rural area contribute significantly to protect Consumer Behaviour and Consumer Rights.  Healthcare in Rural India and Consumer Protection Act, Advertising and Consumer in rural India are important dimensions need to be critically examined to protect Rural Consumerism.

 OBJECTIVES: 

  • 1.      To bring together National experts in Consumer Law, Consumer Rights, Consumer behavior, academicians and representatives of NGOs on a common platform to discuss various issues relating to rights of Rural Consumers in India.
  • 2.      To promote understanding of inter-relationship between Consumer Laws and Protection of Consumers through institutional arrangements.
  • 3.      To catalyze potential synergetic applications to rights of Rural Consumers.
  • 4.      To focus on current state of Rural Consumers in terms of buying behavior and grievance redressal at the back drop of legal and legislative measures.
  • 5.      To prioritize on research, policies and practices those are relevant for facilitating policy makers and programme executives for planning a mechanism in managing appropriate consumer grievance redressal mechanism in rural area.
  • 6.      To explore different dimensions of Consumer Behaviour in rural area as co-related with gender issues.
